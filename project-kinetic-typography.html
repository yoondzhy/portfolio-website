Overview

Translating music into motion and type

This project is a kinetic typography lyrics video created for Yel’s “About Last Night.” I wanted to explore how motion, timing, and typographic treatment could carry not just the words of the song, but also its mood and emotional pacing.

I was drawn to the track because of its calm but emotionally layered atmosphere. The goal was to create a visual language that felt intimate, rhythmic, and expressive without overpowering the music itself.

Process

Exploring tempo, tone, and emotion

01
Song selection
I chose “About Last Night” because its chill atmosphere and emotional subtlety felt well suited to a typography-based motion piece. Instead of relying on dramatic visuals, I wanted to see how far type alone could carry feeling.
02
Mood and pacing
Before animating, I focused on the rhythm of the lyrics and the emotional changes across the track. This helped me decide where motion should feel restrained, where it should expand, and where text should hold tension or softness.
03
Typography in motion
The core challenge was to make typography behave like a performance element. Scale, spacing, entry timing, and transitions were all used to reflect tone, tempo, and emphasis in the song rather than just display lyrics on screen.
04
Refinement
I refined the piece by adjusting timing and transitions so that the animation would feel emotionally aligned with the audio. The final result aimed to balance readability with expressive motion.
Reflection

What I learned

This project taught me that motion design is not only about making elements move, but about making them move with intention. Even small changes in timing or easing can completely change how a scene feels emotionally.

It also made me more aware of typography as something performative. Letters can act almost like voices or gestures when they are paced carefully and given the right visual weight.

Challenge

Balancing expression and clarity

The most difficult part was keeping the work expressive without making it overly busy. Because kinetic typography depends on movement, it can easily become distracting if every lyric tries to demand attention at once.

I had to think carefully about when to emphasize a phrase and when to let the song breathe. That balance between readability and atmosphere became the most important design decision in the project.
