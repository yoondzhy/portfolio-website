Overview

Visualizing a group built on fluid connections

This project explores NCT U as a network rather than a fixed unit. Instead of focusing on discography, I wanted to show how members connect, reappear, and form new combinations across different releases and eras.

The final poster maps these changing relationships through a chord-diagram-based system, emphasizing both continuity and variation — how some members become recurring connectors while others appear in more specific or temporary combinations.

"The most interesting data story is not always the biggest or most complete one. Narrowing focus from all NCT-related data to member relationships made the structure — and its meaning — visible."

Design Decisions

Three questions that shaped the structure

Each design decision came from asking what the data was really trying to say — and whether the visual form was earning its complexity.

01 · Data Selection
What is the right unit of analysis?
I chose member co-appearances over release metrics because the structure of NCT is its most distinctive feature — not its sales. The data had to support an argument, not just represent a corpus.
02 · Visual Encoding
How do you show change over time in a static form?
Rather than animating, I structured the poster around four discrete years — 2016, 2018, 2020, 2022 — treating each as a network snapshot. Density and arc thickness carry the temporal story.
03 · Audience
Who is this for, and what do they need to understand?
The challenge was balancing legibility for outside viewers with enough specificity to be meaningful to fans. The chord diagram is the compromise — complex enough to reward attention, structured enough to read at a glance.
Process

From broad fandom data to a clearer network narrative

01
Topic framing
I started from the wider category of K-pop and NCT, considering sales, streaming performance, unit history, and overall activity. The project was information-heavy, but the story still felt too general — the data existed without a thesis.
02
Feedback and redirection
Professor feedback pushed me to focus on members rather than discography. That shift helped surface the real design question: how can I make NCT U's changing combinations visible in a way that feels intuitive and visually engaging?
03
Testing multiple concepts
I prototyped a hand-drawn member network, an all-member activity heatmap, and a Mark-centered timeline. These drafts helped me compare what was analytically interesting versus what was strong enough visually to carry a full poster.
04
D3.js implementation on Observable
I built the network graph programmatically in D3.js using Observable notebooks, which allowed rapid iteration on force layout parameters and color encoding before finalizing the static poster composition in Illustrator.
05
Final system
Circular network visualizations compare major NCT U phases across four key years. I focused on combinations, recurring members, and shifts in density, then refined composition in Illustrator to balance hierarchy, explanation, and visual impact.
Reflection

What I learned

This project taught me that the most interesting data story is not always the biggest or most complete one. Narrowing focus from all NCT-related information to member relationships made the visualization far more specific and meaningful.

I also learned how important iteration is in data visualization. The early concepts — heatmap, Mark-focused timeline — were valuable not because they became the final work, but because they helped clarify what structure and storytelling this project actually needed.

Challenges

What was hard

The hardest part was finding a format that could handle NCT's complexity without becoming unreadable. Many layouts became too crowded or failed to justify why certain members appeared where they did.

Another challenge was balancing fan knowledge with clarity for outside viewers — staying specific enough to be credible while remaining legible as a general explanation of connection, scale, and change.
