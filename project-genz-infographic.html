Overview

A story that doesn't spare anyone

This infographic explores doomscrolling as a universal experience — not just a Gen Z problem. It traces how a habit that feels harmless at first quietly becomes something heavier, shaping mood, sleep, and attention.

The visual language leans into the downward pull — typographic hierarchy and layout choices that mirror the act of scrolling itself.

How it was made

[28.10.2025]

Exact idea: Doomscrolling trap: how Gen Z’s attention is captured and drained

1. The Attention economy behind addictive social media:
    • ways in which platforms compete for time not value —> screentime, phone pickups, ad exposure

2. Mechanism behind it = Dopamine Looop:
    • boredom feels unbearable, focus feels like withdrawal
    • mechanism to constantly pull you back in and keep scrolling
    • the inventor of short video and scrolling mechanism

3. Emotional drain = consequences:
    • low self-esteem due to comparisons
    • desensitization to global stress

4. reclaiming focus - suggestions/solution:
    • setting scroll limits
    • designing feed mindfully
    • embracing boredom
    • deep work recommendation (Cal Newport) PLanning:

Step 1: What’s your goal?
    • Make people aware of doomscrolling problem
    • The effects of doomscrolling because it is disrupting out lives and decreasing our focus
    • Doomscrolling = stolen attention

Step 2: Who is your audience? Gen-Z, parents of Gen-Z, millenials, generations
    • What makes them tick? —> daily problem

Step 3: What is your story?
    • Visual explanation of mechanisms that take advantage of human vulnarabilities to trap you into doomscrolling, its consequences and ways to help

Step 4: What is your message?- stop doomscrolling and advertize mindfullness
    • Message: understand that doomscrolling isn’t just your problem, but rather companies’ workaround into trapping you based on your human vulnarabilities.

Step 5: How do you find stories in your data?
    • Through structured information

Step 6: What is your supporting content or data?
    • Supporting content: graphs showing the severity of doomscrolling problem to those who think it can’t be that bad; central image, explanations to those visual content.

Final Poster
Reflection

What I learned

This project taught me how much a layout's direction can carry meaning. The vertical descent wasn't just aesthetic — it made people feel the subject.

Through continuous iteration and feedback, I was able to come up with a layout that effectively conveys the message. Each of my drafts were immensely different from each other, and the final one was a result of learning from the mistakes of the previous ones. This project taught me to pay attention to the details of spacing and typography, as well as stucturing information in a way that is engaging and easy to follow.

Challenges

What was hard

Coming up with the layout, the central message, making that central message eyecatching, balancing the information, being mindful of every element.
