Overview

A visual narrative from randomly selected images

This booklet was created from images randomly gathered from our class's shared image pool, then transformed into a cohesive visual narrative. The challenge was to find rhythm, contrast, and surprising relationships between unrelated material.

Rather than forcing a linear story, the design focused on editorial pacing through sequence, spacing, typography, and spread composition that felt spontaneous yet intentional.

Process

From random images to cohesive narrative

01
Image pool selection
Started with randomly selected images from class pool, looking for visual motifs, repeated shapes, color relationships, and mood connections that could create continuity.
02
Spread composition
Designed with center fold always in mind. Two-page relationships needed to feel intentional rather than like separate single-page posters placed side by side.
03
Typography rhythm
Used layout and typography to create moments of pause, contrast, and surprise. Goal was exploratory reading experience that still felt coherent and paced.
04
Digital adaptation
Translated printed booklet into interactive flipbook format to preserve spread-based reading experience online while maintaining the editorial feeling.
Reflection

Key takeaways

Learned how much narrative power comes from sequencing alone. Even unrelated image fragments become meaningful through deliberate layout decisions about rhythm and progression.

Realized spread design requires thinking in pairs, not just individual pages. The relationship between facing pages creates half the reading experience.

Presentation choice

Why flipbook format

Unlike image gallery, flipbook preserves the sequential reading and page-turning sensation. This maintains the editorial intent and makes spread relationships immediately visible.

For spread-dependent work, presentation format becomes part of the work itself rather than neutral documentation container.
